# HR & Workplace Compliance Reference

## Key Topics
- Sexual harassment & workplace harassment (Title VII, EEOC)
- Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI)
- Workplace safety (OSHA)
- Retaliation & whistleblower protections
- Accommodation requests (ADA, FMLA)
- Code of conduct & professional behavior

## Common Scenario Types
- A manager makes an uncomfortable comment to a direct report
- A colleague receives unwanted messages on a work platform
- An employee witnesses harassment but isn't the target
- An employee fears retaliation for reporting misconduct
- A request for reasonable accommodation is made

## Key Terminology
- **Quid pro quo**: Conditioning employment benefits on tolerating unwanted behavior
- **Hostile work environment**: Pervasive behavior that creates an intimidating or offensive workplace
- **Protected characteristics**: Race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, genetic info
- **Bystander intervention**: Actions third parties can take to interrupt or report misconduct
- **Retaliation**: Adverse action against someone for reporting or participating in an investigation

## Regulatory Citations (US-focused)
- Title VII of the Civil Rights Act (1964)
- Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA)
- Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)
- Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA)
- OSHA General Duty Clause

## Scenario Realism Tips
- Set scenarios in recognizable work contexts: meetings, Slack/Teams, performance reviews, off-site events
- Include both obvious and ambiguous situations — ambiguous ones make better learning
- Show what a correct report/escalation path looks like
- Don't just show the violation — show the reporting and resolution process
